About NCC

Brief History

The National Cadet Corps (NCC) is the Indian military cadet corps with its roots tracing back to the British-era University Corps established in 1917. Formally established post-independence in 1948, NCC has evolved to become a premier youth organization in schools and colleges.

Objectives

  • To develop character, comradeship, discipline and a secular outlook among youth.
  • To provide basic military training to cadets.
  • To create a pool of motivated youth to provide leadership in all walks of life.

Benefits for Cadets

  • Leadership skills and confidence building.
  • Adventure camps and opportunities to participate in national events.
  • Civil awareness, fitness, and social service exposure.
  • Enhanced career prospects in armed forces and other government sectors.
